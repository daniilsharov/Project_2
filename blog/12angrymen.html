About the Movie

Title: 12 Angry Men

Release Year: 1957

Director: Sidney Lumet

Starring: Henry Fonda, Lee J. Cobb, Martin Balsam

IMDb Rating: 9.0/10

Synopsis

12 Angry Men, released in 1957 and directed by Sidney Lumet, is a courtroom drama set almost entirely within the confines of a jury room. The film revolves around a jury of 12 men deliberating the fate of a young defendant accused of murder. Initially, all jurors except Juror #8 (played by Henry Fonda) are convinced of the defendant's guilt. However, Juror #8 is not so sure and begins to question the evidence and the motivations of the others, leading to heated debates that reveal the prejudices and biases of the jury members.

12 Angry Men is a powerful exploration of justice, morality, and group dynamics, with a strong emphasis on the importance of reasonable doubt. The film is widely regarded as one of the greatest American films ever made and remains a timeless study of human behavior.
