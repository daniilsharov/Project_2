About the Movie

Title: The Godfather

Release Year: 1972

Director: Francis Ford Coppola

Starring: Marlon Brando, Al Pacino, James Caan

IMDb Rating: 9.2/10

Synopsis

The Godfather, released in 1972, is a crime drama film directed by Francis Ford Coppola. The film is based on the novel by Mario Puzo and follows the powerful Corleone family, headed by Don Vito Corleone (played by Marlon Brando). It explores the complex world of organized crime, loyalty, and betrayal as Michael Corleone (Al Pacino) becomes reluctantly drawn into the family's criminal empire.

Widely regarded as one of the greatest films ever made, The Godfather is known for its iconic performances, gripping story, and themes of power, family, and morality. The film won three Academy Awards, including Best Picture, and has left an indelible mark on popular culture.
